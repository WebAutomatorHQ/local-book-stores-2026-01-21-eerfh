About Local Book Stores

Local Book Stores began as a passion project by Canadian readers who were frustrated with how hard it was to find accurate, city-specific bookstore information while traveling. After too many nights spent scrolling through generic map apps and outdated directories, we started building a focused, human-centric way to explore Canada’s bookstores. What began as a simple spreadsheet of favourite shops in Toronto and Montreal quickly evolved into a nationwide directory—curated through conversations with booksellers, local communities, and avid readers from coast to coast.

Our mission is to make it effortless for anyone in Canada to find and support local book stores in their city. We believe that bookstores are more than retail spaces; they are cultural anchors, gathering places, and gateways to new ideas. Every feature we build is guided by three core values: clarity, community, and care. Our vision is a connected network of Canadian book lovers who can easily discover, visit, and champion the independent and chain stores that keep reading culture vibrant in every neighborhood.

Frequently asked questions

Answers to common questions about how Local Book Stores works, how we list shops, and how you can make the most of the platform.

Local Book Stores organizes bookstores by Canadian city rather than relying solely on generic map searches. You simply select or search for your city, and we present a curated list of local book stores in that area. Each entry is structured to highlight the store’s neighborhood, focus (such as indie, used, children’s, or specialty), and notable features so you can quickly decide which shops to visit without sifting through unrelated results.
We believe readers should have a complete view of their options, so we include both independent bookstores and well-known chains across Canadian cities. Independent shops are clearly highlighted, and we pay special attention to stores that support local authors, host community events, or offer unique experiences. This balanced approach lets you choose when you want the intimacy of a neighborhood shop and when you prefer the convenience of a larger chain—all within a single, easy-to-browse directory.
If you own or manage a bookstore in Canada and would like to be added or have your details updated, you can reach us directly at contact@fredericsune.com. Include your city, address, website or social links, and a short description of what makes your store unique. We review submissions to ensure accuracy and alignment with our local-first focus, then update the relevant city listing so readers can find you quickly.
Using Local Book Stores to search for shops in Canadian cities is free for readers. We do not sell books directly; instead, we focus on connecting you with the local bookstores that do. Our role is to provide a clear, up-to-date, city-based directory so you can decide where to buy in person or online from stores that match your preferences. This lets us stay neutral, highlight a wide variety of shops, and keep the spotlight firmly on the Canadian bookstores themselves.
