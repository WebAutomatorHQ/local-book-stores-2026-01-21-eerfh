Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Local Book Stores (“we”, “us”, or “our”) collects, uses, and protects your information when you use our website and related services. Because our platform focuses on Canadian readers and bookstores, we aim to handle your data with transparency and care in line with applicable privacy laws.

1. Introduction

Local Book Stores operates an informational directory that helps users discover local book stores in Canada by city. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard information in connection with your use of our website, including when you browse our pages, use search features, or contact us.

By accessing or using our website, you agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with this policy, please do not use the site. We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time, and any changes will be posted on this page with an updated “Last updated” date.

Last updated: January 21, 2026

2. Information we collect

We collect a limited amount of information in order to operate, maintain, and improve the Local Book Stores platform. The information we collect generally falls into the following categories:

2.1 Information you provide to us

When you choose to interact with our website, you may voluntarily provide information, including:

  • Contact details: such as your name, email address, and city when you use our contact form or email us directly.
  • Message content: the text of any messages or inquiries you send, including feedback, partnership requests, or listing updates.
  • Business information: if you represent a bookstore, this can include your store’s name, address, website, social links, and descriptive details you choose to share for listing or update purposes.

2.2 Automatically collected information

When you visit our website, certain information may be collected automatically by our hosting provider and analytics tools (as applicable). This information typically includes:

  • IP address and approximate geographic region (city or country level, where available).
  • Browser type, device type, operating system, and basic technical details.
  • Pages visited, referring URLs, time and date of access, and basic usage patterns.

This information is generally collected and used in an aggregated or pseudonymous form and is not used to directly identify individual users, except where required for security or legal reasons.

2.3 Cookies and similar technologies

We may use cookies and similar technologies to support basic functionality, enhance user experience, and understand how visitors use the site. For more details, see the “Cookies and similar technologies” section below.

3. How we use your information

We use the information we collect for legitimate purposes related to operating, securing, and improving the Local Book Stores platform. Typical uses include:

  • Providing and maintaining the website: Ensuring that our pages load correctly, search features function, and content remains available and up to date.
  • Responding to inquiries: Using your contact information to respond to questions, feedback, bookstore submissions, or partnership requests.
  • Improving the platform: Analyzing aggregated usage data to understand which pages are most frequently visited, which cities are searched, and how we can provide clearer and more relevant listings.
  • Maintaining security: Monitoring, investigating, and preventing activities that may violate our terms, compromise security, or be fraudulent or abusive.
  • Legal and compliance purposes: Complying with applicable laws, regulations, and legal processes, and responding to lawful requests from public authorities where required.

We do not sell your personal information to third parties. Any sharing of information is limited to what is necessary to operate our website (for example, hosting providers or analytics services) or to comply with legal obligations.

4. Cookies and similar technologies

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by websites you visit. They are widely used to make websites work, improve performance, and provide usage information to site owners. Depending on your browser settings and our implementation, we may use a few basic categories of cookies:

4.1 Types of cookies we may use

  • Strictly necessary cookies: These cookies are essential for the website to function properly (for example, basic security and network management). You cannot typically opt out of these cookies without affecting core functionality.
  • Performance and analytics cookies: These help us understand how visitors use the site (such as page views or referral sources) so we can improve content and user experience. Data from these cookies is generally aggregated and not used to personally identify you.

4.2 Managing cookie preferences

You can usually manage or disable cookies through your browser settings. Depending on your browser, you may be able to:

  • Block all cookies or only certain types of cookies.
  • Delete existing cookies stored on your device.
  • Set preferences for how cookies are handled in the future.

Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality or performance of our website.

5. Data sharing, retention, and international considerations

We may share limited information with trusted third-party service providers that help us operate our website (for example, hosting, email services, or analytics tools). These providers are only permitted to process information on our behalf and in accordance with this Privacy Policy and applicable law.

We retain personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law. When information is no longer required, we will take reasonable steps to delete, anonymize, or securely store it.

Because our infrastructure or service providers may be located outside of your province or country, your information may be transferred and processed in other jurisdictions. Where applicable, we take reasonable steps to ensure that any such transfers comply with relevant data protection requirements.

6. Your privacy rights and choices

Depending on your location and applicable laws, you may have certain rights in relation to your personal information, which can include:

  • The right to request access to the personal information we hold about you.
  • The right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • The right to request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal obligations.
  • The right to object to or restrict certain types of data processing.

To exercise any of these rights (where available), please contact us using the details in the “Contact us” section below. We may need to verify your identity before responding to certain requests.

7. Third-party websites and services

Our website includes links to external websites, including bookstore websites, platforms, blogs, and social media pages. We provide these links for convenience and informational purposes only. We do not control and are not responsible for the content, privacy practices, or security of those third-party sites.

When you follow links to external websites, any information you provide is governed by the privacy policies and terms of those third parties. We encourage you to review their policies before submitting any personal information.

8. Contact us

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, our data practices, or your privacy rights, you can contact us at:

Email contact@fredericsune.com

When contacting us about privacy, please include enough detail for us to understand your request (for example, whether you are a reader or bookstore owner, and which information your question relates to). We aim to respond within 2–3 business days, subject to request volume and complexity.
